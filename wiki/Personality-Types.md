## Dichotomies Explained

The MBTI is based on four dichotomies:

### Extraversion (E) vs. Introversion (I)

- **E**: Focus on outer world, energized by interaction
- **I**: Focus on inner world, energized by reflection

### Sensing (S) vs. Intuition (N)

- **S**: Focus on concrete facts and details
- **N**: Focus on patterns and possibilities

### Thinking (T) vs. Feeling (F)

- **T**: Make decisions based on logic and objective analysis
- **F**: Make decisions based on values and how they affect people

### Judging (J) vs. Perceiving (P)

- **J**: Prefer structure, planning, and organization
- **P**: Prefer flexibility, spontaneity, and adaptability

## Usage in Character Design

When assigning MBTI types to characters:

1. **Consider their core motivations** - What drives them?
2. **Observe their decision-making** - Logic or values?
3. **Note their energy source** - People or solitude?
4. **Analyze their approach** - Structured or flexible?

**Example Characters:**

- **Dardan Niveli** - INFP (idealistic, values-driven, empathetic leader)
- **Hasan Eyletmez** - ESTP (action-oriented, spontaneous, lives in the moment)
- **Tifa Luenava** - ISFJ (caring, dutiful, protective)
- **Claude Luenava** - INTJ (strategic, analytical, independent thinker)

---

**Note:** MBTI types are a tool for understanding character personality, not a rigid definition. Characters can display traits outside their primary type depending on situation and character growth.
